The Most Underrated Secret To Study

Everyone knows it, yet no one does. I, having stumbled upon it, am willing to share it with you in this short but hopefully transformative blog post.

08-12-2024 [Academia]

Most of us look at studying as this brick wall that one has to either climb, or (if one is strong enough), break through. I absolutely disagree.

It is a tragedy that so many people with such great potential never get the chance to rise, just because of this one misunderstanding.

The things mentioned in this post are not necessarily specific to the process of studying and can be applied to just about anything that involves hard work and working at the very edge of your abilities.


The Killer Misunderstanding

Nearly everywhere, I've heard about how you can "force yourself" or "trick your brain" into studying hard to get good grades. While these methods may work, I don't think using them is going to get me anywhere in the long run.

Until not long ago, I had been thinking the same. Where has that got me? Absolutely nowhere. I consider the progress I made in the previous years insignificant. Vanishingly small.

Why do people think that studying is some kind of inevitable chore? Why can't we just... enjoy the process?! That's right. Studying can be just as enjoyable as sports or music or just about anything else.


Take Two

Alright, here's the big part. Drop all your previous assumptions about study. It'll do you good to just forget what studying (whatever that means) is for a while.

I am no expert, but I'll try my best to get you up to speed. Get ready to rewire your brain!
