Greetings, fellow nerds. I've started learning Rust recently, which is why I've started working on "Archer", a ray tracing engine built to run smoothly even on old hardware.

Let me take you on a short "walk" down the rabbit hole of ray tracing, while I venture out in my quest to build the best ray tracing engine on the planet.

"What's so special about Archer?"

Good question! Well, there must be thousands of ray tracing projects out there, and I acknowledge that many are a billion times better than mine. I'm making this project for three good reasons:

  • I just want to learn Rust hands-on
  • Ray tracers is just too good to avoid
  • Most tracers ain't light enough for my potato

Also, Archer will be built with further buildability, modularity, extensibility, and hackability in mind. For a rough outline of what the project's boilerplate will look like, I decided to follow Peter Shirley's Ray Tracing in One Weekend.
